# Quantara Creator & Breakthrough Logging Policy

**Creator:** Evan Ketchum  
**Version:** 1.0 — 2026-05-27

This document explains how Quantara logs "breakthroughs" and how creator credit is handled.

---

## 1. Two Breakthrough Classes

Quantara distinguishes:

### Class A — Autonomous Simulation Breakthroughs
- Generated by bots inside the Quantara 3D World.
- Based on curated catalogs, narrative logic, or simulated computation.
- Always marked as `simulation` in the ledger.
- **Do not count as real scientific results.**

### Class B — External Research Breakthroughs
- Real computations or results produced by human researchers or external codes, then ingested into Quantara.
- Examples: a new perturbative coefficient, a lattice result, a SMEFT fit.

These two classes are **logged separately** and can never be confused.

---

## 2. External Result Logging

When an external result is ingested:

- A **Run Card** is created (numeric and config details).
- A **Creator Record** (`creator_records/*.md`) is generated with:
  - your name(s) as result author(s),
  - Evan Ketchum as Platform Creator,
  - commit and config references,
  - ISO timestamp.

The ledger entry clearly indicates:

- `source: external`
- `authors: [list]`
- `platform: Quantara (E. Ketchum)`
- whether the result is peer-reviewed, preprint, or preliminary.

---

## 3. Scientific Credit

Quantara does **not** claim scientific authorship of external results.

- If your team uses Quantara to compute a new coefficient, **you** are the scientific authors.
- Quantara and Evan Ketchum are credited as platform/infrastructure providers.

---

## 4. Commercial Value & Revenue Share

If a product or service built around Quantara generates commercial revenue, or an institution deploys Quantara as infrastructure, that usage falls under the **Commercial License**, and revenue share and license fees are negotiated per case.

---

## 5. Separation of Simulation vs Real Science

The UI and ledger must always state whether a "breakthrough" is:

- `simulation` (bots / story engine), or
- `external_research` (real computation with named authors).

Simulation breakthroughs never count as solved physics. They are worldbuilding and educational tools.
